About Me

I'm a Global Business student at HKUST with a focus on sustainability and social impact. My core belief is simple: business should be a force for change, not just profit. Everything I work on connects back to that.

I co-founded Zester, a Hong Kong social enterprise that makes SDG education genuinely engaging through a gamified card game, a digital learning platform, and a youth innovation competition. Zester has reached 30,000+ students across schools and corporations including HSBC, BNY, and TTI, and was recognised as the top 6 best student startups globally at the De La Vega Global Entrepreneurhsip Awards (out of 500K+ students and 32,000 teams), and at the JA Asia Pacific Company of the Year out of 81,000+ students.

Alongside Zester, I intern at HKSTP's Ecosystem Development and Partnerships team, consult on real client projects through ICC at HKUST, and volunteer as a policy researcher at Liber Research Community. I'm drawn to the intersections of sustainability, strategy, and innovation ecosystems, and always looking for where business can drive the most meaningful change.

June 2026

Last week I had the chance to join a management consulting taster programme. Three days, real client briefs, tight deadlines. I went in expecting to learn about slide decks and frameworks. What I actually learned was how much of consulting is about asking the right question before you answer anything. The teams that struggled weren't the ones with weaker analysis — they were the ones that ran hard in the wrong direction. The discipline of pausing to challenge your own assumptions before diving in is something I'm bringing back to everything I do at Zester and ICC.

June 2026

First week at HKSTP done. The scale of what's happening at Pak Shek Kok is genuinely striking — hundreds of startups, labs, and deep-tech teams all in one place, many of them working on problems I had no idea were being tackled at this stage. What surprised me most is how relationship-driven the ecosystem is. The formal structures matter, but the informal connections between founders, corporates, and institutions seem to do a lot of the actual work. Excited to dig into the Ecosystem Development and Partnerships side of things over the coming months.

May 2026

Zester just crossed 30,000 students reached. When we started, the goal was to make SDG education feel less like a checkbox and more like something that actually sticks. A card game felt absurd at the time. Now it's one of the things schools keep coming back for. The bigger lesson from year one: distribution beats product in early-stage social impact. We spent too long refining before we put things in front of real students. Every piece of feedback we got from actually running sessions was worth more than weeks of internal iteration. That's the thing I'd tell anyone starting something similar.

December 2024

The SDG Innovation League wrapped up last weekend. 250+ students, 50+ teams, one very long Saturday. Watching secondary school students pitch genuinely creative solutions to real sustainability problems — and defend them in front of a panel — was something else. A few teams had done primary research that put some university-level work to shame. The hardest part of organising it wasn't the logistics; it was resisting the urge to over-engineer the framework and just letting the students run with it. We're already thinking about how to scale the next edition.
